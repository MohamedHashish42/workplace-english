# **11. Workplace Communication Phrases**

## Key Phrases

* **Do you need any help?** → Offering support.  
  *Example:* *Do you need any help with the report?*

* **Can you give me a minute?** → Asking for a short delay.  
  *Example:* *Can you give me a minute? I’ll be right with you.*

* **I’m in the middle of something.** → Explaining you are busy at the moment.  
  *Example:* *Sorry, I can’t talk now, I’m in the middle of something.*

* **I will be with you shortly.** → Promise to respond soon.  
  *Example:* *I will be with you shortly after I finish this call.*

* **What are you working on?** → Asking about someone’s task.  
  *Example:* *Hey Mohamed, what are you working on today?*

* **To cancel a meeting** → End or stop a planned meeting.  
  *Example:* *We need to cancel the meeting scheduled for tomorrow.*

* **To book a meeting** → Arrange a new meeting.  
  *Example:* *Let’s book a meeting for next week.*

* **To check availability** → See if someone is free.  
  *Example:* *I’ll check your availability before sending the invite.*

* **To confirm** → Make sure the plan is fixed.  
  *Example:* *Can you confirm if you received my email?*

* **To re-arrange** → Change the meeting time/date.  
  *Example:* *Let’s re-arrange the meeting for Monday instead of Friday.*

* **Could you update me on your progress?** → Asking for a status update.  
  *Example:* *Could you update me on your progress with the project?*

* **Let’s set up a meeting.** → Suggesting a new meeting.  
  *Example:* *Let’s set up a meeting with the client tomorrow.*

* **Let’s coordinate our schedules.** → To compare our free time and find a suitable appointment.  
  *Example:* *Let’s coordinate our schedules for next week.*

* **Can we discuss this further in our meeting?** → Postpone discussion to a meeting.  
  *Example:* *Can we discuss this further in our meeting tomorrow?*

* **I will follow up with you on this later.** → Promise to return with an answer.  
  *Example:* *I’ll follow up with you on this later today.*

* **I appreciate your patience.** → Polite when asking someone to wait.  
  *Example:* *Thank you, I appreciate your patience while I finish this task.*

* **Let’s touch base.** → To **talk briefly** to check progress or get an update.  
  *Example:* *Let’s touch base after the meeting to finalize details.*

* **Please let me know if you need any assistance.** → Offering support.  
  *Example:* *Please let me know if you need any assistance with the task.*


## ✍️ Practice Activities

### **Role-Play Dialogue**

**Context:** Mohamed and Ahmed are colleagues working on a project.



**Mohamed:** Hi Ahmed, do you need any help with your tasks?

**Ahmed:** Thanks, Mohamed. Actually, **can you give me a minute? I’m in the middle of something.**

**Mohamed:** Sure, no problem. **Let me know when you’re free.**

**Ahmed:** Okay… done! So, **what are you working on** right now?

**Mohamed:** I’m updating the client presentation. By the way, **could you update me on your progress** with the report?

**Ahmed:** Almost finished, I’ll send it this afternoon.

**Mohamed:** Great! **Let’s set up a meeting** tomorrow to review everything.

**Ahmed:** Good idea. Should we **book a meeting** room or just do it online?

**Mohamed:** Let’s book a room. I’ll **check availability** and **confirm** with you.

**Ahmed:** Perfect. But I think we need to **re-arrange** the client meeting from Friday to Monday.

**Mohamed:** I agree. I’ll **cancel** Friday’s meeting and send a new invite.

**Ahmed:** Thanks, **I appreciate your patience** while I finalize the slides.

**Mohamed:** No worries. Let’s **coordinate our schedules** and make sure we’re both free.

**Ahmed:** Okay. **Can we discuss this further in our meeting** tomorrow?

**Mohamed:** Absolutely. Let’s **touch base** after the meeting too, just to make sure we’re aligned.

**Ahmed:** Perfect. And please **let me know if you need any assistance** with your part.

**Mohamed:** Will do. **I’ll follow up with you** later today with the updated file.
